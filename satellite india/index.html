What Is Satellite Internet?

Satellite internet is a type of internet connection that uses communication satellites in space to transmit data between a user’s dish antenna on the ground and an internet service provider. Instead of using cables (like fiber or broadband), it sends and receives signals through satellites orbiting Earth.

Key Points

Works in remote or rural areas where cables can’t reach.
Requires a satellite dish and modem.
Faster now with new systems like Starlink.
Extended Information

Satellite internet delivers connectivity using communication satellites that orbit Earth. A dish antenna at your location sends and receives signals to a satellite, which communicates with ground stations connected to the global internet. This system allows users in remote locations to access the internet even where fiber or cable networks cannot reach.

Key Features
  • Wide Coverage: Works in remote, hilly, forest, and island areas.
  • No Wiring Needed: Requires only a dish and modem.
  • Higher Latency: Signals travel long distances to satellites.
  • Modern Improvement: Starlink’s LEO satellites reduce delay dramatically.
  • Weather Sensitive: Heavy rain or storms may reduce quality.

This makes satellite internet ideal for regions with limited infrastructure or where traditional wired broadband is unavailable.
